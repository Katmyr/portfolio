Dogliday

CONTEXT

Independent musicians spend 50% of their time on non-creative tasks. Since 82% of them manage their technical logistics entirely alone, the setup phase becomes a major point of friction, causing a 30% time loss before the first note is even played

GOALS

- Automate the sound engineering process for small venues.
- Reduce the technical entry barrier for the 76% of bars that struggle to host live music.
- Give time back to the artist by streamlining the "Plug-and-Play" experience.

DELIVERABLES

- Experience Map: Detailed user journey.
- Hardware & App: Modular robot interface and its companion mobile application.
- 3D Modeling: High-fidelity prototyping developed in Blender.
- Motion Design: Promotional video and interface interactions rendered in After Effects.

Context & Methodology

Dogliday addresses a powerful emotional challenge: how can you entrust your dog to a loved one without guilt or stress? For this project, the UX focus was primarily on reassurance. The goal was to create a continuous information bridge between the owner and the "dog-sitter" so that separation is no longer a source of anxiety.

Ideation & Prototyping: From Paper to Click

My process began with paper wireframes to lay the foundations of navigation without being influenced by aesthetics. These sketches were quickly transposed into low-fidelity prototypes on Figma for user testing. This crucial step allowed us to validate flow clarity (booking, health records) and iterate on the Information Architecture before moving to the high-fidelity phase.

VALIDATION

Testing core user flows early in the process.

ITERATION

Refining IA based on real user feedback.

Design System & Consistency

The major asset of this project is its comprehensive Design System. Every button, icon, and spacing was designed to be scalable, allowing for the addition of new features without ever breaking visual consistency. This ensures a seamless brand identity across all touchpoints.

Validation: Final Heuristic Analysis

Once the interfaces were finalized, I subjected the design to a strict heuristic audit based on Norman & Nielsen criteria. This final step validated system status visibility (e.g., dog health notifications) and user control (e.g., easy booking modification). This approach guarantees a frictionless experience, even for novice users.

Heuristic CriterionImplementation
Visibility of System StatusReal-time health alerts
User Control & FreedomEasy booking edits/cancel
Consistency & StandardsUnified Design System

Communication & Motion Design

To support the launch, I created a promotional video using Motion Design in After Effects. The objective was to translate the app's "easy and playful" nature into movement, reinforcing the brand identity through fluid and reassuring animations.
