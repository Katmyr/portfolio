Landmark

CONTEXT

Independent musicians spend 50% of their time on non-creative tasks. Since 82% of them manage their technical logistics entirely alone, the setup phase becomes a major point of friction, causing a 30% time loss before the first note is even played

GOALS

- Automate the sound engineering process for small venues.
- Reduce the technical entry barrier for the 76% of bars that struggle to host live music.
- Give time back to the artist by streamlining the "Plug-and-Play" experience.

DELIVERABLES

- Experience Map: Detailed user journey.
- Hardware & App: Modular robot interface and its companion mobile application.
- 3D Modeling: High-fidelity prototyping developed in Blender.
- Motion Design: Promotional video and interface interactions rendered in After Effects.

Context & Challenges

Working for a market leader like TCC Global required rethinking retail loyalty. The current issue? Programs are too transactional. Customers are no longer loyal to a brand; they have become opportunistic, chasing promotions rather than values.

Strategy & Positioning

We began by analyzing TCC Global's positioning to understand how to inject emotional engagement. The goal was to shift from a customer who simply "consumes" to a member who actively "participates."

GOAL

Moving from transactional to emotional loyalty.

METHOD

In-depth brand positioning analysis.

Insights & Final Design

The final solution is based on a "phygital" journey: in-store challenges that reward eco-responsible or community engagement rather than just the total on the receipt.
