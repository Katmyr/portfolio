Maestro

CONTEXT

Independent musicians spend 50% of their time on non-creative tasks. Since 82% of them manage their technical logistics entirely alone, the setup phase becomes a major point of friction, causing a 30% time loss before the first note is even played

GOALS

- Automate the sound engineering process for small venues.
- Reduce the technical entry barrier for the 76% of bars that struggle to host live music.
- Give time back to the artist by streamlining the "Plug-and-Play" experience.

DELIVERABLES

- Experience Map: Detailed user journey.
- Hardware & App: Modular robot interface and its companion mobile application.
- 3D Modeling: High-fidelity prototyping developed in Blender.
- Motion Design: Promotional video and interface interactions rendered in After Effects.

Specifications & Solution

I wanted to flip the current paradigm: the venue should adapt to the instrument, not the other way around. The device meets three core requirements:

UNIVERSALITY

Compatible with all instruments (including vocals).

ACCESSIBILITY

Intuitively usable, regardless of technical expertise level.

SPEED

"Plug-and-play" configuration for immediate deployment.

DEVICE FEATURES APP FEATURES
  • Gain
  • EQ (Bass/Mids/Treble)
  • Primary Effects (Reverb, Delay, Distortion, Tremolo)
  • Master Volume
  • Acoustic Calibration
  • Setlist
  • Sound inputs (Combo XLR/6.35mm Jack Connector)
  • Validate / Next / Previous
  • Auto-track change (after set duration + 10s silence)
  • Additional Jack input for pedal
  • Detachable module for bar robot connection
  • Pedal effect settings (per song)
  • Full robot effect mirroring
  • Panning
  • Compression
  • Noise gate
  • Setlists management
  • Validate
  • Automatic levels (per song)

Research & Ideation

I placed all of these features on the two devices : the speaker and the tablet (which is part of the speaker but can be detached).

I developed a CMF (Colors, Materials, Finishes) mixing reassuring analog codes with modern ergonomics. These concepts served as the foundation for a rapid ideation phase on Vizcom, allowing me to explore several aesthetic directions while maintaining user interface consistency.

3D Process

While Vizcom was a powerful lever for quickly exploring aesthetic intentions, I chose to switch to Blender for the final phase. This transition to traditional 3D modeling was essential to regain total control over ergonomic precision and technical detail fidelity that AI could not yet formalize with accuracy. This project was also an opportunity to deepen my modeling skills to ensure a high-fidelity prototype, true to my initial vision.
